# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

University students (NUS and similar) who use Canvas LMS daily and are tired of clicking through folders to download lecture files. They find this page from the GitHub repo, a portfolio link, or a friend. Context: procrastinating at a desk, deciding in under 30 seconds whether this tool is worth ten minutes of setup. Secondary audience: recruiters and fellow devs skimming the creator's work.

## Product Purpose

canvas-scraper is a Python CLI that syncs Canvas course files to your machine, skips the junk (videos, textbooks, 500MB lecture recordings), and emails a daily digest of new announcements and assignments. The hero page exists to (1) explain the payoff in one breath, (2) get the visitor to the GitHub repo with the setup command in hand, (3) collect stars. Success: visitor stars the repo or copies the setup command.

## Brand Personality

Lazy-genius. Witty, self-deprecating, efficiency-obsessed ("Lazy to click buttons? Me too."). The smugness of someone who automated a chore and now naps while it runs. Three words: drowsy, smug, precise. Emotions to evoke: recognition ("that's me"), amusement, mild envy.

## Anti-references

- SaaS template slop: gradient heroes, fake dashboard screenshots, pricing-page energy, icon card grids, "Trusted by" logos.
- Hacker-terminal cliché: green-on-black, scanlines, matrix energy. It's a tool for lazy students, not a heist.
- Canvas LMS's own branding (institutional red/white). This page is the antidote to Canvas, not an extension of it.

## Design Principles

1. **The page practices the laziness it preaches.** One scroll, one command, no navigation, nothing to configure. If the visitor has to work, the brand is broken.
2. **Show the artifact, not the abstraction.** The daily email digest is the product's proof of life; render it, don't describe it.
3. **Comfort over cool.** Warm, sunlit, afternoon-nap energy. The tool works at noon so you don't have to.
4. **Jokes earn their place.** Wit in headlines and microcopy, never at the cost of clarity about what the tool does.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast. Respect prefers-reduced-motion (entrance reveals collapse to opacity or nothing). Semantic HTML, keyboard-reachable links, real text everywhere (no text-in-images).
